Is there an easier way to look at climate data?
Building scientists and architects use weather data collected from weather towers to inform their designs. Having knowledge of the climate can reduce energy loads and making it easier to employ passive designs. Climate information is readily available as epw files (which is like a big spreadsheet of different environmental factors for a site). Usually different software is needed to visualize these different points, but even the visualizations can be difficult for people in the field to understand, sometimes requiring 20 page articles about how to read a chart. How much more for a student?
The main goal of this project is to develop a tool for building scientists, architects, and architecture students that can easily describe what a climate is like. This project combines all of my interests, architecture, data, programming, and ui/ux.

How can we turn stress into something positive?
The main goal of this project is design a wearable and a matching app to track and turn stress into something positive. However, Wearables are seen as being kind of nerdy and are highly obvious. They don't attract people who aren't into tech or health. For this project, the target audience are young college-aged or high school girls.
In addition, this project pays attention to the interface's aesthetics as a part of the experience. It should be noted that technical logistics of the wearable were not an element to consider.

Can user-generated data be a substitute for
site analysis?
Twitter-sentiment analysis is both qualitative and quantitative. I've spent about a year slowly diving into twitter sentiment analysis from using online sites that showed maps of where tweets were located to learning how to scrap my own twitters and diving into the digital humanities?
Why? Because I wanted to see what information we could really get and I wanted to see what the data could be used for (also because once upon a time I sat, read, and organized a hundred or so surveys and thought about whether using python would be more efficient (would it even be accurate?)).

Ultimately, text analysis is best for exploring data sets.
